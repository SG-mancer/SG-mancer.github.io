## Task 1: Assessment redesign with AI

This week's task directed us to use generative AI to redesign an existing assessment task for greater accessibility.

The following chat is with [Duck.ai](https://duck.ai/). I selected it for its privacy advantage, as it anonymizes interactions and promises not to use your data for training, and seemed a thoughful application of [GTP-5 mini](https://developers.openai.com/api/docs/models/gpt-5-mini). ## Task 2: Using technology for assessment in the classroom

[OneNote Class Notebook (with Microsoft Teams)](https://www.onenote.com/classnotebook) is a useful tool for formative assessment because it provides a low-friction way for teachers to spot-check student work live and leave direct comments in each student's private notebook. This immediate, visible feedback loop—without the need to collect, copy, or resubmit physical books or digital documents can help all students improve their understanding during the lesson itself.

In the case of the lesson used in [week 11](img/11lessonplan.pdf), the end-of-lesson production journal writing could be checked immediately by the teacher, or even during class for those students who finish their work early. This immediate access allows the teacher to provide timely reminders about the required scaffolding for each journal entry, which asks students to clearly explain three key elements: "What you did, how you did it, and why you did it (or what this means for future tasks in your project)." Rather than waiting until after the lesson or until books are collected days or weeks later, the teacher can intervene while the student is still actively writing and thinking about their work.

This approach incorporates an even tighter feedback loop than the checkpoints suggested in the above assessment redesign. Because feedback is delivered during the lesson itself, every student is provided with a genuine opportunity to read the teacher's comments, make immediate adjustments to their writing, or seek further clarification from the teacher. This prevents students from repeating misunderstandings across multiple entries and ensures that feedback directly shapes the quality of the final submitted product, rather than simply justifying a grade after the fact.
